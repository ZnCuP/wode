    MAP sensors are critical for engine performance. Common signs that indicate a failing MAP sensor include:

    • Poor fuel economy
    • Rough idling or hesitation during acceleration
    • Check engine light illumination
    • Black smoke from exhaust
    • Engine stalling or difficult starting

    Our MAP sensors are designed to OEM specifications and provide reliable performance. For detailed technical specifications and installation guidance, please contact our technical team.

    Oil level sensors and oil pressure sensors serve different functions:

    Oil Level Sensor: Measures the amount of oil in the oil pan. It warns you when oil levels are low, preventing engine damage from insufficient lubrication.

    Oil Pressure Sensor: Monitors the pressure of oil circulation in the engine. It ensures proper oil circulation and warns of potential pump or blockage issues.

    Our oil level sensors are precision-engineered for accurate readings and long service life. For more information about sensor compatibility with your vehicle, please visit our products page.

    DPF sensors typically last 80,000-120,000 miles under normal driving conditions. However, replacement frequency depends on:

    • Driving conditions (city vs highway)
    • Fuel quality
    • Engine maintenance
    • DPF system health

    Signs that your DPF sensor may need replacement include frequent regeneration cycles, DPF warning lights, or reduced engine performance. Our DPF sensors meet strict emission control standards.
